# Milestone v0.9 — Validation, Repair, and Reporting Consolidation

## Status

**Completed**

## Scope and Intent

v0.9 is a policy consolidation and hardening milestone. The goal is not to expand capability, schema, or UI, but to make existing backend behavior explicit, deterministic, and curator-trustworthy when confronted with real-world GEO metadata.

This milestone focuses on clarifying and stabilizing:

* validation rules
* repair triggering boundaries
* terminal vs non-terminal fallbacks
* flagging and reporting semantics

No learning, memory, or schema changes were introduced.

---

## Baseline

* v0.8 behavior is frozen and treated as correct
* Canonical output schema remains fixed at 8 fields
* Ontology grounding remains deterministic and auditable
* LLM usage remains gated, conservative, and cache-aware

---

## What Changed in v0.9

### 1. Policy Formalization

Observed behavior across many real-world GEO datasets was converted into **explicit policy**, rather than implicit heuristics. This includes:

* Disease generalization rules for sloppy or underspecified disease strings
* Handling of model names (e.g. CT26 mouse tumor model)
* Clear separation of *accept*, *flag*, and *repair* conditions
* Explicit treatment of non-anatomical tissue placeholders

All policies are now documented in `docs/policies/policy-spec.md`.

---

### 2. Disease Handling Improvements

* Deterministic disease generalization added for common cancer descriptors (e.g. "lung tumor" → "lung cancer")
* Model identifiers and genotype descriptors are no longer silently coerced into disease ontology terms
* Explicit handling of `Healthy`, `Healthy Donor(s)`, and similar phrases
* Low-confidence ontology matches remain **flagged**, not force-repaired

---

### 3. Tissue Type Disambiguation

* Clear separation between **anatomical tissue** and **disease labels** used incorrectly as tissue
* Non-anatomical tissue values (e.g. `Tumor`, `Lymphoma`) are treated as terminal fallbacks with explicit flags
* No attempt is made to infer anatomical site when evidence is insufficient

---

### 4. Repair Loop Hardening

* Repair triggers are strictly limited to semantic or ontology failures
* No repair is attempted for known terminal placeholders
* Missing repair templates no longer cause spurious failures when deterministic fallback is intended

---

### 5. Reporting and Audit Clarity

* Flags vs failures are clearly distinguished
* Curator-facing flags are conservative and meaningful
* Audit logs preserve full traceability of decisions
* Final decision (`ACCEPT` vs `FLAGGED`) aligns with curator expectations

---

## Out of Scope (Intentionally Deferred)

* Cross-dataset disease inference
* Learning or memory across runs
* UI redesign or curator workflow changes
* Aggressive ontology normalization

---

## Known Limitations

* GEO metadata quality varies widely and cannot be fully normalized automatically
* Some disease and tissue ambiguities require human curator judgment
* Ontology coverage gaps remain in edge cases

These are surfaced via flags rather than hidden heuristics.

---

## Deliverables

* `docs/policies/policy-spec.md` — authoritative policy description
* Multiple policy tickets (#84–#99) consolidated into documented behavior
* Updated checkpoint reflecting current backend behavior

---

## Conclusion

v0.9 establishes a stable, explicit, and reviewable policy layer for geo-gsm-annotator-agent. Future milestones can now build on this foundation with confidence, using documented policy rather than inferred behavior.
